Women Through-out History

These women have inspired countless number of us.

Rani Laxmi Bai

Rani Laxmi Bai was an Indian queen and warrior who fought against British rule during India's First War of Independence in 1857. She is remembered for her bravery, patriotism, and leadership in battle, and is often referred to as the "Queen of Jhansi."

Learn more

Abigail Adams

She was a strong advocate for women's rights and education, and was one of the first women to urge her husband to consider women's rights in the formation of the new American government. In a letter to her husband, she famously wrote, "Remember the Ladies, and be more generous and favorable to them than your ancestors.

Learn more

Harriet Tubman

Born into slavery in the United States, Tubman became a leader of the Underground Railroad, a network of secret routes and safe houses used to help slaves escape to freedom. She also served as a nurse, cook, and spy during the American Civil War.

Learn more

Toshiko Akiyoshi

She has broken down barriers for women and minorities in the music industr.Akiyoshi's music has been described as "brilliantly original," and she is considered to be one of the most important and influential jazz musicians.
